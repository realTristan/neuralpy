World War I, also known as the Great War, was a global conflict that took place from 1914 to 1918. It was one of the deadliest and most significant wars in history, marking a turning point in the course of human events. This essay explores the causes, key events, and lasting impacts of World War I, shedding light on how it shaped the modern world we live in today. Causes of World War I:
Several complex factors contributed to the outbreak of World War I. The immediate cause was the assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand of Austria-Hungary in June 1914. However, underlying causes, such as militarism, alliances, imperialism, and nationalism, had been brewing for years.
